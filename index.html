Top 20 Trends Affecting Essex

We are living longer

By 2035, the number of people aged over 85 will be more than double

Our population is getting more diverse

Over-spill from London into Essex will lead to a mix of new cultures and people with different backgrounds

More of us will work from home

Trends point to 5.5 million homeworkers in the UK by 2030. In Essex, 95% of all premises will have access to superfast broadband by 2019.

We will need different and new skills during our working life

Digital skills and creativity will be keys skills of the future but 1 in 5 employers in Essex currently can’t find people with basic IT skills

More of us will work for ourselves

10% of people in Essex are already self-employed and this number is expected to increase significantly, especially for young people

More of us will rent our homes than buy them

By 2025, the UK will have more private renters than mortgage holders

House prices will continue to rise

London over spill and better transport connections in Essex will likely see Essex house prices rise over the next decades

Our roads and railways will get busier

Essex already has the 3rd highest road use in Britain, which is expected to increase by 32% across the UK in the next 15 years

We will rely more on renewable energy

Two-thirds of existing power stations in the UK are set to close by 2030

Temperatures will rise due to climate change

Climate change will impact our countryside, changing patterns of wildlife and agriculture

Our homes will be more at risk of flooding

More frequent extreme weather events will increase the risk of flooding in Essex, particularly in South Essex and along our coastline

More of us are becoming obese

1 in 5 children in Essex could be obese by 2030

Hospitals will be under increasing pressure

More care will be provided in the community to help hospitals cope with increased pressure

More of us will need social care

By 2030, the number of people needing social care in Essex is expected to quadruple

We will have more control over our health care

Health services will be more personalised, with more people being able to decide how they spend their own health budget

Types of crime will change

Cybercrime will increase drastically, as will awareness of domestic violence and crimes against the person

Our lives will become more reliant on the internet and our devices

The number of connected, intelligent and autonomous devices could reach 100 billion by 2020

Our data will become increasingly valuable

By 2030, data will become part of nearly every service we interact with, changing the way business and government are run

Digital technology will transform public services

Digital technologies could empower front line staff and citizens to co-create public services in new, easily accessible and inclusive ways

Funding for our public services will be more local

Councils have had a real spending reduction of 32% since 2010 and face a £14.4 billion funding gap by 2019/20

Essex is going to change a lot over the next 20 years. By 2035, the number of people aged over 85 will more than double. More of us will work from home, with some 5.5 million of us doing so by 2030. More of us will rent our homes than buy them. Our roads and railways will get busier – Essex already has the third highest road use in Britain and this is expected to increase by 32 per cent over the next few decades.

But whatever Essex looks like, we want to make sure it’s still the Essex we all love. Partners from across Essex have got together and we want your help in developing a shared vision for Essex.

There will be a further chance to get involved and share your thoughts about the vision for Essex next year.
